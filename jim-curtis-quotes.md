# Jim Curtis: Quotes from Podcast & Media Appearances

Compiled from YouTube transcripts across multiple interviews and his TEDx talk.

---

## On Fear and the Unknown

> "The biggest stressor in life, the biggest fear in life is the unknowing. And when you can get comfortable with unknowing, you lower the inflammation in your energy field, your mind, and then your body."

> "Bringing real awareness that your fear is lying to you. That's not true. It's not true at all."

> "Fear lies. Most of the time, fear is lying. Unless there's a lion coming at us, then we know to run."

> "What would you do if you weren't afraid? If you weren't afraid what people were going to say, if you weren't afraid of losing, if you weren't afraid of failing, how would that change things?"

> "We fear the unknown and it cripples us. The cure for that fear of the unknown is curiosity."

> "Fear lives in our thoughts. And then it manifests in our body."

> "If you're not afraid, you don't need balance. If you're not worried this is going to happen again, all you're doing is stepping into love and having fun. Instead of looking for balance, I would lower fear."

## On Identity and the "I Am"

> "The I am really is the underlying identity of who we are. Our reality is made from who we think we are, what we can accomplish. Our identity creates our reality."

> "You are not your circumstances. You are not your trauma. You're not your pain. You're not your upbringing. That's all your identity. And an identity is created. It's a belief."

> "Those things are all constructed. And if they have been programmed into us, constructed into us, they could be reconstructed. It's not set in stone."

> "I am what I want to be, whatever it is. I am calm. I am centered. I am a loving person. We start to say the words, and then I take actions to support that."

> "When we say I am, we believe it. We believe it's part of our ability. Part of our identity."

> "There is a higher self that is listening to our chatter. When we connect more with the higher self, we close our eyes, we can hear all our thoughts. Who is the one that's listening to all that? There's an awareness above all that. And what is that awareness? Well, that's the higher self."

> "I am the ocean in a drop. I'm not the wave. I am the ocean."

## On Programming and the Subconscious Mind

> "We are only programs. When we were born, we didn't have religion, we didn't have sports teams. We didn't have a career. All we knew is that we loved our mother and we needed to survive. Over time, that source energy that made us a baby was programmed with all the beliefs that were around us."

> "The subconscious mind is 95% of the time. The conscious mind thinks it's leading the show, but it's only 5%."

> "You can fight it all day long with the conscious mind and never make a change."

> "The conscious mind has motivation and the subconscious mind has all the habits. You could hustle hustle hustle and it's like this is still not changing. One realization, one aha moment, one unlock and everything opens up for you."

> "All your subconscious mind wants is to keep you safe. It's all it wants to do. It's for your survival. Sometimes it's malinformed. It's dysfunctional. You don't need to be safe from this thing. In fact, it's limiting you."

> "I was programmed with this. So if I was programmed with it, then I can be programmed with other things. Let's change the program."

> "When you have a realization, it's forever."

## On Self-Talk and Reframing

> "Really start to hear what you're saying to yourself and start to question it and then reframe it. If someone's like, 'I'm just stuck,' perhaps it's, 'I am figuring it out.' Or, 'I'm lazy,' perhaps, 'I'm resting right now.' We start to change the conversation because our body is listening."

> "Our subconscious mind is listening. The autonomic nervous system, the habits, the reactions, the reflexes are listening to what we tell it."

> "We're saying so many negative things quietly in our mind that we need to sometimes counteract them with positive ones that we say loudly."

> "The problem is you say your three affirmations in the morning and at night and you think you're doing something good and throughout the rest of the day you're saying I'm stupid, I'm fat, I'm dumb, I'm not lovable."

> "Saying affirmations when you don't believe them is like shooting a BB gun at a piece of steel."

> "Affirmations are like drinking green juice. One green juice isn't going to heal you. And then you go smoke some cigarettes. It's a lifestyle."

> "I'll be walking down the street in New York City and if I trip, if I have a lot of pain, I hear myself saying like, 'God damn this body,' shaming my body. And I'll stop and yell on the street, 'No, you're doing good, buddy.' Literally I'll counteract that negative comment with a positive one out loud on the street."

## On Enough-ness

> "The biggest switch for all of us, what stems all of our anger, all of our fear, all of our sadness is usually around 'I'm not enough.' Because if I knew that I was enough, I wouldn't care about these things that made me angry. I have plenty coming to me. I have so much abundance. I have so much worthiness. I am enough of it all."

> "We have been programmed from an early age that we weren't enough. That's the biggest unlock."

> "In this society it's been indoctrinated to us that we're not enough, and so we have to go out and try to find ways to compensate for that."

## On His Origin Story and Illness

> "I woke up one day and I couldn't feel my left foot. Started to move up to my calf and to my knee, to my leg, and to my waist."

> "I went all over the world. Everybody from the Mayo Clinic to Johns Hopkins to Cleveland Clinic to Ecuadorian Shaman, Grandmasters of Kung Fu. I even wrote a book about this."

> "No diagnosis. And that created so much fear and anxiety. And I realized that whether I have a diagnosis or not, life persists whether I can walk or not. And if I then die, that's okay, too."

> "It wasn't until I realized that I could go within and accept a few things and then look out with love. Connect more to the loving feeling that eradicates fear that all my pain went away."

> "I still walk with a limp today, but I don't hate myself anymore. And because of that, I love everyone else that has an issue."

> "Without this illness, without this limp, without this fear that I wasn't lovable, without the abusive father, I would not have a laser focus ability to understand you and help you change."

> "Some people call it hypervigilance. I call it a superpower."

> "Every issue, every trauma, every obstacle is for your highest good."

## On Childhood and Hypervigilance

> "My father being very passionate and my mother being very stoic was like oil and water. And so as I was growing up, I would just wait for the explosions. I was constantly watching and waiting and so bracing for impact."

> "I watched my father just so I could know how to react to his insanity and that gave me an ultimate superpower. It gave me an ultimate superpower of being able to see people before they even see themselves."

> "Many of us as kids are grown up with parents who were just kids having us. As soon as we started to jump on the table or express our energy or to rebel in some way, what happened? We were told, 'No, that's wrong. Don't.' And we started to believe that."

## On Curiosity and Taking Steps

> "I never stopped being curious and exploring. Even when I was hopeless and I didn't want to get out of bed. There was still something that said I'm curious about what could be."

> "You take one step and the next step follows. You don't have to take all the steps. You just take one and the next step follows. Your feet will never fail you."

> "Give yourself permission. Give yourself permission to just be unhappy doing it. Give yourself permission to fail. Give yourself permission to be exactly where you are right now. And then give yourself permission to take another step out of curiosity."

> "Even if a door closes, a window opens. And sometimes it's pitch black in the hallway, but hang in there."

> "Radically accept that's the way it is and that it's okay. And now from a place of a little bit of more ease, what are the one step that I can take? Because I don't have to solve this tomorrow."

## On The Divine Masculine

> "The divine masculine is grounded. It has roots. It's all the way down. It doesn't have to show off. It doesn't have to be showy. It doesn't have to jump. It doesn't have to shout. The divine masculine is an oak tree. It's a redwood."

> "The divine masculine is supposed to settle your nervous system as soon as you get in touch with it."

> "What is the identity that I need to be most successful and happiest in life? And the identity came: just be the oak, be the tree."

## On Manifestation and Action

> "Manifestation is the expression of intention and action that brings about a result."

> "I see in the spiritual and wellness world, people will sit and meditate and magically make what they want appear. It could. But Prince Charming is not coming into your living room. You got to go out there and put yourself in a position where you might meet Prince Charming."

> "If you're not taking action every day, that man's not going to show up in your living room if you've never left the house."

> "We can step back and focus on the outcomes we want as opposed to focusing on the outcomes that we don't want."

## On the Yellow Butterfly Exercise

> "Challenge your subconscious mind to find a yellow butterfly. Put it on your bedside table. When you wake up in the morning, say, 'I'm so grateful for all the yellow butterflies I'm going to see today.' The middle of the day, say, 'I wonder where these yellow butterflies are.' And then let me know when you start to see them everywhere in your life."

> "We're training our activating system, telling our subconscious mind to go out and find yellow butterflies."

## On Gratitude

> "Things immediately lift when you look at what you have instead of what's missing."

> "Gratitude is like drinking more water. If you actually drink eight glasses of water a day, you'll see your skin and your body and everything change."

> "Writing three in the morning and three at night is like doing three push-ups in the morning and three at night. Throughout the day, every time a negative feeling comes up, you need to switch to gratitude. That's what builds the muscle."

> "Forgiveness is only for self. You got to forgive yourself or else you're stuck."

> "I'm sorry. Please forgive me. Thank you. I love you." (The Ho'oponopono prayer)

> "The more times you say that out loud to yourself, the more you change the source code of forgiveness, the more you change the frequency of it."

## On Emotional Regulation

> "I'm not one thing all the time. I am a dynamic person and I don't have to live in the four emotions of fight, flight, anger, sadness, fear. I'm a human being with 4,000 emotions."

> "Having real power is to say emotions come and go. I can emotionally regulate and I can use any emotion. I can feel the whole bandwidth of emotions because that's okay. But also they don't control me."

> "I had a problem expressing anger because my father was always so angry that if I got angry then he would rage. It's not until I found my anger that I was like, oh, this is useful."
