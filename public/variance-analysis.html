Overview

What this document does

The product spec outlines the day-by-day app experience. The strategic research (briefing, thesis, and playbook) outlines what market data says works. This page maps where they agree, where they diverge, and what the research suggests each divergence means for conversion, retention, and revenue.

Note: This is not a judgment on the content quality. The daily program content (meditations, install phrases, emotional arcs) is strong and differentiated. The variances identified here are structural and commercial: trial length, pricing, paywall timing, and content visibility strategy.

Variance 01 · Highest Risk

Visible-but-locked content

The spec says paid content should be "ALL CLEARLY VISIBLE BUT NOT ACCESSIBLE." The research warns against this specific pattern. Of all the variances, this one carries the greatest risk to brand trust and legal exposure.

TL;DR Every wellness app that locked visible content now has an F rating on BBB or sub-2.0 on Trustpilot. Zero exceptions.

What the FTC says about this pattern

Active regulatory crackdown

The FTC's 2024 study of 642 apps found 76% use at least one dark pattern and 81% make it impossible to turn off auto-renewal during signup. The FTC's Click-to-Cancel final rule and California's Automatic Renewal Law amendments (effective July 2025) are tightening enforcement.

The briefing documents $7.5M in ARL settlements from predatory subscription practices.

The research recommends a different approach

The briefing's free tier strategy: "28% of all sessions unlocked. Enough to build trust without giving away the core product."

The iron rule: "Never interrupt a session mid-play."

The conversion philosophy: "The conversion driver is not frustration. It is desire." And: "The soft gate plus 17 free sessions gives users a reason to convert from desire, not frustration."

Industry median free-to-trial conversion: 7% (Calm's current rate). Research target: 10-15% using emotional onboarding plus generous free tier.

The alternative: Let users experience the full 14-day arc. Convert them at the natural emotional peak (Day 14: "do you want to continue becoming?") rather than gating content to create FOMO.
The pattern is clear. Every wellness app that moved to visible-but-locked content saw short-term revenue gains followed by trust collapse, regulatory exposure, and brand damage that took years to repair. The competitors who did this are now the cautionary tales cited in FTC enforcement actions.

+18,700 additional subscribers per 100K trials. That is a 69% higher conversion rate for the longer trial. The research also identifies the #1 churn reason: "No habit formation (40%)". A 14-day arc directly addresses this by establishing the habit before asking for payment.

The emotional timing problem

The spec's Day 7 paywall lands on "The Space Between Selves," which the spec describes as: "Awareness cannot be undone. You are no longer fully unconscious." This is a moment of emotional uncertainty.

The research suggests asking for payment at the moment of maximum investment (Day 14), not maximum uncertainty (Day 7).

Variance 04 · Retention

Missing: Morning Routine Anchor

The spec provides detailed session content but does not anchor the experience to a specific time of day. The research identifies this as a critical retention mechanism.

TL;DR Users who set time-of-day reminders retain at 3x the rate. 80% drop off within 30 minutes without structure.
3x
Higher Retention
Users who set reminders vs. those who don't
80%
Drop-off Rate
New users within 30 min without structure

The thesis positions the entire product as: "designed to rewire how someone starts their morning."

The briefing uses "history, mood, time of day, pillar balance" as smart recommendation inputs. The playbook specifies "exercise variety, time-of-day patterns, and pillar history combine into push notifications written for one person."

Retention data shows only 3.9% of users remain by Day 15 without structured engagement. Morning anchoring directly combats the Day 1 to Day 15 retention cliff (69% → 3.9%).

Suggestion: The spec's daily content is strong. Adding a consistent time-of-day framing (e.g., "your morning session" or "tonight's session") would align the content with the habit-formation strategy documented in the research.
