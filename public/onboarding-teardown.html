The Math
Every step costs 20% of users.

Research from Gabor Cselle and product analytics across thousands of apps: each additional onboarding step loses roughly one in five users. The math is unforgiving.

THE CATCH

Step count alone doesn't determine survival. Perceived relevance is what matters. Endel's 20 steps work ($750K/mo revenue) because each question visibly connects to the personalized soundscape the user receives. Our 9 steps work because each one earns the next. Open's 14 steps fail because 11 of them are brand narrative the user didn't ask for.

The Insight

Every competitor treats onboarding as a funnel to optimize. The question is whether we treat it as the first meditation to experience.

OUR TAKE

Launch with A, A/B test against E, let the data decide. All five share the same post-onboarding sequence: first session before home screen, daily reminder prompt (Calm's 3x retention insight), and push notification soft-ask (91.1% vs 43.9% baseline).

"The question is not how many screens. It is whether each screen earns the right to ask for the next one."
The Onboarding Thesis
Design Principles
Six rules. No exceptions.
01
Emotion before information
First two screens contain zero data collection. They exist only to create a feeling.
02
Every screen earns the next
No screen assumes the user will continue. Screen-to-screen completion should exceed 90%.
03
Stillness is a feature
5-second delay on screen 01. Intentional processing pause on screen 06. Silence communicates confidence.
04
No dark patterns, ever
Free option presented with same visual dignity as premium. No grayed-out text, no guilt language, no "are you sure?" screens.
05
Animation as communication
Every animation has a purpose. Nothing moves without meaning. Celebration bursts mark real moments, not decoration.
06
Account creation comes last
2 to 3 minutes of emotional investment before asking for an email. Login walls backfire because they demand info before trust develops.
Three Iron Rules
1 No notification permissions during onboarding. Defer until after first completed session. Soft-ask yields 91.1% vs 43.9%.
2 No health or biometric permissions. Endel asks 4 in a row. We ask zero.
3 First session before home screen. Headspace's pattern. Highest first-session conversion in the category.

Executive Summary
What we learned from tearing apart
every major competitor.
What fails
  • Brand narrative before value (Open's 11 screens)
  • Permission spam during onboarding (Endel's 4 in a row)
  • Paywall before any value (Calm at step 5)
  • 100% content locking (Headspace)
What works
  • Value during onboarding (Endel's ambient sounds)
  • First session right after paywall (Headspace's breathing)
  • Scholarship/free option on paywall (Waking Up's trust)
  • Compressed lifecycle emails (Calm's 4x revenue)

Five options that combine every winning pattern while avoiding every mistake. The recommendation is Option A (stillness-first, 9 screens) with Option E (hybrid, 7 screens) as the A/B test. But the right answer comes from data, not conviction.

The right flow is the one
that earns the next screen.

Five options. One shared truth: every screen must justify its existence. Launch with a hypothesis, test with data, and let users tell you which first impression converts.
