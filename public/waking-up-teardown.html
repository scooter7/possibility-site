Company Profile
The philosopher's meditation app.

Waking Up is not a wellness app. It is a philosophical project dressed in iOS skin. Founded by Sam Harris (Stanford undergrad, UCLA neuroscience PhD, 5x NYT bestseller, 30+ years practice). ~38 employees, HQ: Encino, CA. Fully bootstrapped, zero VC. Donates 10%+ via GiveWell.

Evolution Timeline
2014
Book: "Waking Up" published
Sam Harris lays the intellectual foundation. Meditation as consciousness exploration, not stress relief. The book becomes a NYT bestseller and creates the audience.
2018
App Launch
Launches with a 28-day introductory course and a free scholarship model from day one. Differentiates from Headspace/Calm by refusing to gamify. No streak counters. No badges. No celebration screens. Only 5 free sessions before the paywall.
2020
Theory Section Added
Conversations with experts, deep lectures on consciousness, free will, and the nature of self. The app becomes a university, not just a meditation tool.
2021
Guest Teachers Program
Joseph Goldstein, Adyashanti, Henry Shukman, Diana Winston, Alan Watts, Yuval Harari, and others. Expands beyond Harris's singular voice. Content library explodes.
2022
Moments & Practice Expansion
Adds "Moments" for micro-practices throughout the day. Expands into life philosophy content. Blurs the line between meditation app and intellectual platform.
2025
NYT Wirecutter Pick & $12M/yr Revenue
Reaches ~$12M+ annual revenue with $0 advertising spend. All growth organic through podcast, editorial, and word of mouth. 2M+ lifetime downloads, ~48K/mo.

Onboarding
01
Intellectual Credibility
Sam Harris is a neuroscientist with a PhD from UCLA. Every word in the app is backed by decades of personal practice and academic rigor. You can't fake this. Calm has celebrity narrators. Waking Up has a philosopher who has sat with Buddhist monks in Nepal for months.
02
No Gamification
No streaks. No badges. No "Great job!" screens. This is a radical choice in a market where every competitor uses behavioral psychology to create dependency. Harris treats users as adults. The app respects your intelligence.
03
Scholarship Model
Can't afford $129.99/year? Just ask. Free year, no questions asked. No income verification. No guilt. No friction. This single decision generates more trust than any marketing campaign could. It also neutralizes the "meditation shouldn't cost money" objection.
04
Depth Over Breadth
While Calm adds sleep stories narrated by Matthew McConaughey, Waking Up adds conversations about the nature of consciousness with leading philosophers. They chose a lane and committed. The audience that finds them stays forever.
05
Guest Teacher Program
Joseph Goldstein, Adyashanti, Henry Shukman, Diana Winston, Alan Watts, Yuval Harari. These are the most respected meditation teachers and thinkers alive (and some beyond). Harris uses his platform and reputation to bring them directly to users. No other app has this roster.
06
Theory + Practice Integration
Most apps separate learning from doing. Waking Up weaves philosophical understanding into the practice itself. You don't just meditate; you understand why each technique exists and what it reveals about the mind. This creates practitioners, not consumers.
"The goal of meditation is not to become a good meditator. The goal is to recognize the nature of consciousness itself."

Weaknesses
Where Waking Up fails.
And this is where we win.

Every strength becomes a weakness when taken too far. Waking Up's intellectual rigor creates real barriers to adoption, retention, and growth. These aren't bugs. They're structural vulnerabilities.

Strengths (Become Vulnerabilities)
Intellectual rigor becomes inaccessibility. The app introduces nondual awareness by Day 14. Reviewers recommend "start with Headspace first." Only 1 in 10 users reach nondual competence.
No gamification means no habit architecture. Users who need external motivation (most humans) simply stop opening the app.
Depth-first approach creates a learning curve that feels like homework for the 80% who just want to feel calmer.
Sam Harris dependency means the brand is inseparable from one person's voice, opinions, and public controversies.
Pure Weaknesses
Cold onboarding. Drops you into a 28-day course with zero personalization. No "what brought you here?" No adaptive paths.
Content overwhelm. Hundreds of sessions, lectures, conversations with no clear progression system. Where do you go after the intro course?
No emotional design. The UI is clean but lifeless. No delight. No moments of beauty. Meditation is emotional; the app is clinical.
Zero community. No shared experiences. No group sits. No connection features. Meditation becomes isolation.
Growth ceiling: ~48K downloads/mo. Male-skewed, secular, intellectual audience. Limited mainstream and female appeal. Cancelled users lose all progress.

Content Model
How Waking Up organizes content.
And why it creates confusion.

Waking Up has arguably the deepest content library in the meditation app space. Core approach: non-dual mindfulness from Dzogchen, Zen, and Advaita Vedanta. But depth without structure creates paralysis, not progress.

Content Type Volume Quality Discoverability Progression
Introductory Course 28 sessions Excellent Clear path Linear
Daily Meditations 1,000+ Excellent Date-based only No progression
Guest Teacher Series 20+ courses Outstanding Buried in tabs Per-series only
Theory / Lessons 100+ talks Excellent No categorization Random order
Conversations 50+ Excellent Chronological No threading
Moments Growing Very Good Contextual Semi-guided
Key Insight
World-class content, amateur navigation. Waking Up has more meaningful content than any competitor. But finding the right session at the right moment requires the user to already know what they're looking for. That's backwards.
Our Advantage
Guided pathways win. The Possibility can match Waking Up's depth while adding adaptive progression. Imagine knowing exactly which session to do next based on where you are in your practice, not just what day it is.

Steal This
The scholarship model is genius. Free for anyone who genuinely cannot afford it. It costs Waking Up almost nothing (digital marginal cost is zero) but generates massive goodwill. Combined with $0 advertising spend, the entire growth engine runs on trust and organic word of mouth. The Possibility should adopt a similar model, but make it more visible and less buried.
UX & Design
Clean, minimal, and emotionally flat.

Waking Up's design is competent but forgettable. In a category where the emotional experience matters as much as the content, "clean" is not enough.

Waking Up
+ Clean, minimal interface. No visual clutter.
+ Dark mode that's actually well-executed.
+ Consistent typography and spacing system.
- No animation, no transitions, no delight.
- Navigation is tab-based and shallow. Finding specific content requires hunting.
- Timer screen is purely functional. No visual beauty during meditation.
- Post-session screen is a dead end. No suggestions, no reflection, no next step.
- No progress visualization. Users have no sense of where they are in any journey.
The Possibility Advantage
✓ Emotional design: animations, haptics, and visual beauty during and after sessions.
✓ Adaptive navigation that learns what you need and surfaces it.
✓ The three pillars (Release, Align, Become) create an intuitive content architecture.
✓ Post-session reflection with journaling and progress tracking.
✓ Visual progress: constellation map shows where you've been and where you're going.
✓ Sound design and ambient environments that make the app itself feel like sanctuary.
✓ Smart Timer with visual breathing guides and ambient scenes.
✓ Community features: shared sits, group challenges, teacher Q&A.
Lessons to Steal
Six things we take from Waking Up
and make our own.

Good artists borrow. Great strategists study what works, understand why it works, then execute it better within their own framework.

Steal #1
The scholarship model. Adopt it immediately. Make it more prominent than Waking Up does. Put it on the paywall screen, not buried in settings. "If cost is a barrier, it shouldn't be."
Steal #2
Guest teacher credibility. Jim's network of practitioners and teachers is a weapon. Waking Up proved that featuring respected voices alongside the founder multiplies perceived value.
Steal #3
Theory + practice integration. Don't separate learning from doing. Every session in The Possibility should teach something about why the practice works, woven into the experience itself.
Steal #4
Anti-gamification positioning. Market against streaks and badges. "We don't trick you into meditating. We give you reasons to want to." This message resonates with the exact audience Waking Up attracts.
Steal #5
Moments / micro-practices. Waking Up's "Moments" feature is smart. Quick 1-2 minute practices throughout the day. The Possibility should have these, but contextualized: "Moments for anxiety," "Moments before meetings," "Moments for sleep."
Steal #6
Long-form conversations. Waking Up's 60-90 minute conversations with experts create deep engagement and perceived value. The Possibility should feature Jim in similar conversations, establishing him as the intellectual peer of Harris.
Battle Plan
How The Possibility beats Waking Up.

We don't beat them at their game. We change the game. Waking Up wins on intellectual depth. We win on everything else that matters for building a sustainable, growing practice.

01
Win the First 10 Minutes
Waking Up drops you into a course. We drop you into an experience. Before any signup, before any paywall, The Possibility delivers a session that changes how you feel. The onboarding IS the product.
02
Solve the Day 29 Problem
Waking Up's intro course ends and users fall off a cliff. The Possibility never lets the guided path end. Adaptive progressions that evolve with the user. Release, then Align, then Become. Always the next step.
03
Make It Beautiful
Waking Up looks like a textbook. The Possibility should feel like a sanctuary. Visual design, sound design, haptic feedback, ambient environments. The app itself becomes a place you want to be, not just a tool you use.
04
Build What They Won't
Community. Shared experiences. Group sits. Teacher Q&A. Waking Up is a solitary experience by design. Harris believes meditation is inherently solo. We believe transformation is inherently social. That's the gap.
05
Democratize the Depth
Waking Up's depth is only accessible to people with existing meditation knowledge. The Possibility should deliver the same depth, but through a progressive disclosure model. Start simple. Reveal complexity as the user is ready for it.
06
Own the Emotional Layer
Harris teaches you what meditation is. Jim teaches you what it feels like. That's not a small difference. The market is full of people who understand meditation intellectually but can't sustain a practice. Emotion is the bridge between knowing and doing.
The Verdict

Waking Up is the app for people who already meditate. The Possibility is the app for people who want to become someone who meditates. That's a much larger market, and nobody owns it yet.

Respect the competitor.
Then outbuild them.

Waking Up proved that intellectual depth can sustain a meditation app. Our job is to take that depth and make it accessible, beautiful, and impossible to leave. Same substance. Better architecture. Wider door.
